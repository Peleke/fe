# Email 4 — Why your robo-advisor's business model just died

**Trigger**: Day 8 after signup
**Subject**: Schwab just killed the product you're probably using

---

In January 2026, Charles Schwab shut down Intelligent Portfolios Premium. This was their hybrid product: low-cost automated investing plus limited access to a human financial planner. Not cheap enough to compete with Betterment. Not personal enough to justify the fee.

The middle ground between dumb automation and expensive human advice is officially dead.

**What's left?**

Two extremes. On one side: pure robo-advisors (Betterment, Wealthfront) at 0.25% AUM. They pick your allocation, rebalance quarterly, and give you zero customization. On the other: human advisors at 1-2% AUM or $5K+/year. They know you, but they're expensive and slow.

Nothing in between survived. Schwab proved it.

**The robo-advisor problem isn't fees. It's rigidity.**

Morningstar's 2025 digital advice survey found that robo-advisors face higher client churn than traditional advisors, especially during downturns. When markets drop and you can't talk to anyone or change your strategy, you leave.

EPAM's analysis nailed it: "Most robo-advisors restrict investments to predetermined ETF portfolios, limiting clients' ability to access individual stocks or alternative investments."

You can't exclude your employer's stock. You can't tilt toward a sector you believe in. You can't react to a specific event. The algorithm doesn't know you exist.

**Where Fe fits.**

Fe is the layer that sits between you and your brokerage. It's not a robo-advisor — it doesn't take your money and decide for you. It's an agent that understands your positions, monitors the market, and proposes actions. You approve. It executes via eToro's API.

The personalization comes from the agent knowing YOUR portfolio, YOUR thresholds, and YOUR goals. Not from a questionnaire that asks if you're "aggressive" or "conservative."
