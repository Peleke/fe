# Email 2 — The 5 DeFi primitives in 3 minutes

**Trigger**: Day 2 after signup
**Subject**: 5 things happening on blockchains right now (that affect your money)

---

Even if you only hold stocks and ETFs, DeFi is eating into traditional finance fast enough that you should know the vocabulary. Here's the whole landscape in under 3 minutes.

**1. Decentralized Exchanges (DEXs)**
Uniswap, Curve, and others let you swap tokens without a middleman. No brokerage, no order book, no waiting for market hours. Liquidity comes from regular people depositing token pairs into pools. Think of it as a vending machine instead of a stock exchange.

**2. Lending and Borrowing**
Aave and Compound let you deposit crypto to earn interest, or borrow against your holdings as collateral. The rates adjust automatically based on supply and demand. Your savings account at Chase pays 0.01%. Aave pays 2-8% depending on the asset and the day.

**3. Staking**
Some blockchains (Ethereum, Solana) let you lock up tokens to help secure the network. In return, you earn rewards — typically 3-6% annually. It's the closest thing crypto has to a bond: predictable yield in exchange for locking capital.

**4. Liquidity Provision (LP)**
When you deposit a pair of tokens (say ETH + USDC) into a DEX pool, you're a liquidity provider. Every time someone swaps those tokens, you earn a cut of the fee. The risk: if one token's price moves a lot, you can end up with less value than if you'd just held the tokens. This is called "impermanent loss" — and it's one of the things Fe monitors for you.

**5. Yield Farming**
Moving capital between protocols to chase the highest returns. Yield farming was the DeFi gold rush of 2020-2021. Today it's more mature — returns are lower but more sustainable. The complexity is in knowing which protocols are safe and when yields start decaying.
