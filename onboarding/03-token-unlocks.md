**Trigger**: Day 5 after signup

Most price movements in crypto are unpredictable. Token unlocks aren't.

**What's a token unlock?**

When a crypto project launches, most of the tokens don't circulate immediately. They're locked in vesting contracts and released on a schedule — to the team, early investors, and community programs. The dates and amounts are written into the smart contract. They're public.

A "token unlock" is when a batch of those tokens becomes tradeable.

**Why it moves the price.**

Simple supply and demand. If 10% of a token's total supply suddenly becomes available to sell, there's selling pressure. Historical data across hundreds of unlocks shows an average price drop of 8-12% around large unlock events.

Not every unlock causes a dump — sometimes the market has already priced it in, or the recipients don't sell immediately. But the correlation is strong enough that ignoring unlocks is leaving money on the table.

**Real example.**

ARB (Arbitrum) had a 92M token unlock worth ~$142M. If you held ARB, you had three options: hedge with a short position, reduce your spot exposure before the unlock, or hold and accept the risk. The key is knowing it's coming.
